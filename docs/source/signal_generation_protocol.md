# Signal Generation Protocol

## Purpose

Define how candidate signals enter, remain in, and leave the governed signal universe.

## Phase Ownership

- Phase I-G freezes signal-admission policy and proof burden.
- Phase II-0 scaffolds evidence and manifest surfaces.
- Phase IV is the first operational signal-factory phase.

## Protocol Scope

- candidate signal admission requirements,
- identity, versioning, and provenance expectations,
- distinction between admission, validation, and promotion,
- retirement and decay handling,
- alternative-data and non-tabular signal entry rules.

## Guardrails

- Signal breadth is not a free-form expansion lane.
- Event-driven and non-tabular signals require PIT-safe contracts.
- Phase IV is the first phase allowed to become signal-factory-serious.

## Required Outputs

- admission checklist,
- minimum evidence package,
- retirement policy notes,
- links to governed signal-expansion decisions tracked in the ledger.
